\section{Heater Box PLC I/O Interface}

The following tables define the PLC I/O mapping for the Heater Control Box. All signals are routed through the DB37 passthrough interface board.

\subsection{Digital Inputs (5069-IB16)}

\begin{table}[htbp]
\centering
\caption{Heater Box Digital Inputs}
\label{tab:heater-din}
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{l l l X}
\hline
\textbf{IB16 Channel} & \textbf{DB37 Pin} & \textbf{Signal} & \textbf{Description} \\
\hline
Din0 & 1 & Pushbutton 1 & Operator input (e.g., Start / Run) \\
Din1 & 3 & Pushbutton 2 & Operator input (e.g., Stop / Acknowledge) \\
\hline
\end{tabularx}
\end{table}

\subsection{Digital Outputs (5069-OB16)}

\begin{table}[htbp]
\centering
\caption{Heater Box Digital Outputs}
\label{tab:heater-dout}
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{l l l X}
\hline
\textbf{OB16 Channel} & \textbf{DB37 Pin} & \textbf{Signal} & \textbf{Description} \\
\hline
Dout0 & 11 & Green LED 1 & Status indicator (e.g., Power/Run) \\
Dout1 & 12 & Red LED 1 & Status indicator (e.g., Fault/Stop) \\
Dout2 & 13 & Green LED 2 & Status indicator \\
Dout3 & 14 & Yellow LED & Status indicator (e.g., Warning) \\
Dout4 & 15 & Red LED 2 & Status indicator (e.g., Alarm) \\
Dout5 & 16 & Fan & Thermal disturbance / cooling \\
Dout6 & 17 & Heater Relay & Energizes the 150\,\si{\ohm} heater element \\
\hline
\end{tabularx}
\end{table}

\subsection{Analog Inputs (5069-IY4)}

\begin{table}[htbp]
\centering
\caption{Heater Box Analog Inputs}
\label{tab:heater-ain}
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{l l l X}
\hline
\textbf{IY4 Channel} & \textbf{DB37 Pins} & \textbf{Signal} & \textbf{Description} \\
\hline
Ain0 & 20--21 & Temperature & 4--20\,mA from ProSense XTD2 transmitter \\
Ain1 & 22--23 & Potentiometer & 0--10\,V setpoint from front panel pot \\
\hline
\end{tabularx}
\end{table}

\subsection{Analog Outputs (5069-OF4)}

\begin{table}[htbp]

\hline
\end{tabularx}
\end{table}
